Core idea

A prefix is useful if it changes the future.

Existing process reward models often judge whether each intermediate step is locally valid. PUM instead estimates the marginal contribution of a prefix: the improvement in downstream solve probability after conditioning student models on that prefix.

Instead of asking “Is this step correct?” PUM asks “Does this prefix increase the chance of success?”

Method

Estimate gain, construct preferences, train a utility model.

01

Sample prefixes

Collect vertical prefixes from the same trajectory and horizontal prefixes from different trajectories with similar lengths.

02

Measure gain

Lightweight students solve each problem with and without the prefix. The solve-rate difference forms a gain profile across capability levels.

03

Train PUM

Gains are normalized and converted into within-problem pairwise preferences. PUM learns a scalar utility score with pairwise ranking.

PUM-Math

Outcome-grounded supervision without manual step labels.

PUM-Math replaces human step-level correctness labels with gain-based pairwise utility preferences derived from student rollouts. The result is a scalable prefix-supervision dataset for mathematical reasoning.

Results

One utility signal for selection, search, and RL.

PUM can score complete responses as terminal prefixes, guide partial branches during search, and provide dense prefix-level credit assignment during RL.

Best-of-N selection

PUM achieves best or near-best selection accuracy across policy models and datasets, with the clearest advantage at larger candidate pools.

better trajectory ranking

Beam search

PUM consistently improves prefix-level pruning across datasets, policy models, and search budgets, preserving useful partial reasoning branches before final answers are produced.

better partial-branch guidance

Reinforcement learning

PUM provides dense prefix-level credit assignment and remains stable in process-only RL, where PRM and PQM are more vulnerable to reward hacking.

more stable process rewards

Evaluation by intervention

PUM naturally connects with Teach2Eval.

Both works evaluate an object by its effect after intervention. Teach2Eval evaluates a model through student improvement after teaching; PUM evaluates a reasoning prefix through student solve-rate improvement after conditioning on the prefix.
